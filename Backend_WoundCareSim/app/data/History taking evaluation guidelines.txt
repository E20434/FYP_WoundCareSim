HISTORY TAKING GUIDELINES
For Post-Operative Wound Care

================================================================================
PURPOSE
================================================================================

Gather essential patient information before performing wound care to ensure 
safe and appropriate treatment.

================================================================================
PATIENT IDENTIFICATION
================================================================================

FIRST STEP - Always verify patient identity:
• Ask patient to state their full name
• Verify date of birth
• Check patient wristband/ID
• Never assume identity based on bed location

WHY: Prevents treating the wrong patient (serious safety error)

================================================================================
ALLERGY ASSESSMENT
================================================================================

MANDATORY - Ask about allergies:

Questions to ask:
• "Do you have any allergies to medications?"
• "Are you allergic to penicillin or other antibiotics?"
• "Any allergies to tape, adhesives, or latex?"
• "Have you had reactions to dressings or antiseptics?"

What to document:
• The substance (e.g., Penicillin)
• Type of reaction (rash, swelling, difficulty breathing)
• When it occurred

CRITICAL ALLERGIES:
• Antibiotic allergies (especially penicillin)
• Latex allergy (affects glove selection)
• Adhesive/tape allergies (affects dressing choice)

WHY: Prevents allergic reactions during wound care

================================================================================
PAIN ASSESSMENT
================================================================================

Pain Scale (0-10):
• 0 = No pain
• 1-3 = Mild pain
• 4-6 = Moderate pain
• 7-10 = Severe pain

Questions to ask:
• "Where is your pain located?"
• "On a scale of 0-10, how would you rate your pain?"
• "Can you describe the pain?" (sharp, dull, throbbing, burning)
• "Is the pain constant or does it come and go?"
• "Have you taken pain medication today?"

RED FLAGS - Report immediately:
• Sudden severe pain at wound site
• Pain score above 7
• New or worsening pain

WHY: Pain assessment ensures patient comfort and can indicate complications

================================================================================
MEDICATION HISTORY
================================================================================

Questions to ask:
• "What medications are you currently taking?"
• "Have you taken your medications today?"
• "Are you taking any blood thinners?"

Important medications to note:
• Blood thinners (warfarin, aspirin) - increased bleeding risk
• Steroids (prednisone) - slower wound healing
• Blood pressure medications - patient should take as scheduled

WHY: Medications can affect wound healing and procedure safety

================================================================================
SURGICAL HISTORY
================================================================================

Questions to ask:
• "What surgery did you have?"
• "When was your surgery?" (which post-operative day)
• "Who was your surgeon?"
• "Where is the surgical wound located?"

Information needed:
• Type of surgery performed
• Date of surgery
• Location of surgical incision
• Type of wound closure (sutures, staples, etc.)

WHY: Understanding the surgery guides appropriate wound care

================================================================================
MEDICAL HISTORY
================================================================================

Ask about conditions that affect wound healing:
• Diabetes - affects healing, increases infection risk
• High blood pressure - affects circulation
• Smoking - impairs healing

WHY: These conditions require extra care during wound management

================================================================================
COMMUNICATION BEST PRACTICES
================================================================================

DO:
• Introduce yourself: "I'm [Name], a nursing student"
• Use simple, clear language (not medical jargon)
• Make eye contact and listen actively
• Ask open-ended questions: "Tell me about your pain"
• Show empathy: "I understand this may be uncomfortable"
• Explain what you will do: "I'm going to check your wound"
• Ask if patient has questions

DON'T:
• Rush the conversation
• Use complicated medical terms
• Interrupt the patient
• Dismiss patient concerns
• Assume the patient understands

================================================================================
WHAT TO DOCUMENT
================================================================================

Record:
✓ Patient name and ID verified
✓ Allergies (type and reaction)
✓ Pain location and score (0-10)
✓ Current medications
✓ Surgery type and date
✓ Relevant medical history
✓ Any patient concerns

================================================================================
SUMMARY CHECKLIST
================================================================================

Before proceeding to wound care, confirm you have:

☑ Verified patient identity
☑ Asked about allergies
☑ Assessed pain level
☑ Reviewed current medications
☑ Confirmed surgical details
☑ Noted medical history
☑ Answered patient questions
☑ Documented all information

================================================================================
KEY PRINCIPLES
================================================================================

1. SAFETY FIRST - Always verify identity and allergies
2. LISTEN to the patient - They know their body best
3. BE PROFESSIONAL - Maintain respectful communication
4. DOCUMENT EVERYTHING - If not documented, it wasn't done
