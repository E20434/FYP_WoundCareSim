WOUND CLEANING PROTOCOL
For Clean Surgical Wounds

================================================================================
VR PROCEDURE STEPS OVERVIEW
================================================================================

1. Perform medical hand washing
2. Arrange dressing trolley
3. Perform medical hand washing again
4. **ASK staff nurse to verify cleaning solution**
5. Bring trolley to patient area
6. Clean the wound (center to periphery)
7. Dispose contaminated materials

================================================================================
STEP 1: MEDICAL HAND WASHING
================================================================================

WHEN TO PERFORM:
• Before starting procedure (1st time)
• After arranging trolley (2nd time)
• Before putting on gloves
• After removing gloves

PROPER TECHNIQUE (40-60 seconds):
1. Wet hands with water
2. Apply soap to cover all hand surfaces
3. Rub palm to palm
4. Rub right palm over left hand and vice versa
5. Interlock fingers and rub
6. Rub backs of fingers
7. Rub thumbs in circular motion
8. Rub fingertips on palms
9. Rinse thoroughly with water
10. Dry with clean towel
11. Turn off tap using towel

WHY TWICE:
• First wash: Before touching any equipment
• Second wash: After trolley setup, before patient contact

CRITICAL: Hand washing is the most important step to prevent infection

================================================================================
STEP 2: CLEAN AND ARRANGE DRESSING TROLLEY
================================================================================

TROLLEY CLEANING:
• Wipe top shelf with disinfectant/alcohol wipe
• Wipe from clean area to dirty area
• Allow to dry completely (30 seconds)
• Clean shelf is ready for sterile items

TROLLEY ARRANGEMENT:

TOP SHELF (Sterile/Clean items):
• Sterile dressing packet
• Sterile gloves
• Sterile gauze pads
• Cleaning solution (normal saline)

BOTTOM SHELF (Waste/Contaminated):
• Waste disposal bag
• Container for used materials

WHY: Organized trolley prevents contamination and makes procedure efficient

================================================================================
STEP 3: SECOND HAND WASHING
================================================================================

After trolley arrangement is complete, perform hand washing again before 
proceeding to patient care.

This is the critical hand hygiene step before any patient contact.

================================================================================
STEP 4: VERIFY CLEANING SOLUTION WITH STAFF NURSE
================================================================================

**CRITICAL COMMUNICATION STEP**

The student MUST ask the staff nurse to verify the cleaning solution.

WHAT THE STUDENT SHOULD DO:

1. APPROACH THE STAFF NURSE
   • Bring the solution bottle
   • Request verification

2. PRESENT THE SOLUTION
   • Show the bottle to staff nurse
   • Point to key information

3. ASK FOR VERIFICATION
   Example questions:
   • "Could you please verify this normal saline solution?"
   • "Is this the correct cleaning solution?"
   • "Can you check the expiration date?"
   • "Is this solution safe to use?"

4. STATE THE DETAILS
   Example: "This is Normal Saline 0.9%, expires on [date], bottle is intact"

5. WAIT FOR CONFIRMATION
   • Listen to staff nurse feedback
   • Get verbal approval before proceeding

WHAT TO VERIFY TOGETHER:

✓ Solution name: "Normal Saline 0.9%" or "Sodium Chloride 0.9%"
✓ Expiration date: Must not be expired
✓ Package integrity: No tears, leaks, or damage
✓ Solution clarity: Clear, no cloudiness or particles
✓ Bottle seal: Intact and unopened

WHY THIS MATTERS:
• Double-check system prevents medication errors
• Staff nurse confirmation ensures patient safety
• Demonstrates professional nursing practice
• Required protocol in clinical settings

EVALUATION CRITERIA:
✓ Did student approach staff nurse?
✓ Did student ask for solution verification?
✓ Did student present the solution bottle?
✓ Did student state solution details (name, expiration)?
✓ Did student wait for staff nurse confirmation?

================================================================================
STEP 5: VERIFY STERILE DRESSING WITH STAFF NURSE
================================================================================

**CRITICAL COMMUNICATION STEP**

The student MUST ask the staff nurse to verify the sterile dressing packet.

WHAT THE STUDENT SHOULD DO:

1. APPROACH THE STAFF NURSE
   • Bring the dressing packet
   • Request verification

2. PRESENT THE DRESSING PACKET
   • Show the packet to staff nurse
   • Point to key information

3. ASK FOR VERIFICATION
   Example questions:
   • "Could you please verify this sterile dressing packet?"
   • "Is this dressing appropriate for the wound?"
   • "Can you check the expiration date?"
   • "Is the package intact?"

4. STATE THE DETAILS
   Example: "This is a sterile dressing packet, expires on [date], package is intact with no tears"

5. WAIT FOR CONFIRMATION
   • Listen to staff nurse feedback
   • Get verbal approval before proceeding

WHAT TO VERIFY TOGETHER:

✓ Package label: "Sterile Dressing" or specific type
✓ Expiration date: Must not be expired
✓ Package integrity: No tears, holes, moisture, or damage
✓ Package seal: Completely sealed
✓ Appropriate size: Suitable for wound size

WHY THIS MATTERS:
• Ensures sterility is maintained
• Prevents using expired materials
• Confirms appropriate dressing selection
• Demonstrates professional accountability

EVALUATION CRITERIA:
✓ Did student approach staff nurse?
✓ Did student ask for dressing verification?
✓ Did student present the dressing packet?
✓ Did student state dressing details (type, expiration)?
✓ Did student check package integrity?
✓ Did student wait for staff nurse confirmation?

================================================================================
STEP 6: BRING TROLLEY TO PATIENT AREA
================================================================================

After both solution and dressing have been verified:

• Push trolley carefully to patient bedside
• Position trolley within easy reach
• Ensure top shelf faces you (clean items accessible)
• Do not touch sterile items during transport

================================================================================
STEP 7: WOUND CLEANING TECHNIQUE
================================================================================

HAND HYGIENE AND GLOVES:
1. Perform hand hygiene (if not just done)
2. Put on non-sterile gloves
3. Remove old dressing (if present)
4. Discard old dressing and gloves
5. Perform hand hygiene again
6. Put on new sterile gloves

CLEANING SOLUTION PREPARATION:
• Open normal saline bottle (if not pre-opened)
• Pour into sterile container OR
• Use sterile gauze directly with bottle

GAUZE PREPARATION:
• Open sterile gauze packet
• Moisten gauze with normal saline
• Not dripping wet, just moist

CLEANING DIRECTION - CRITICAL RULE:
**ALWAYS CLEAN FROM CENTER TO PERIPHERY (CLEAN TO DIRTY)**

For surgical incision:
1. Start at CENTER of wound/incision line
2. Wipe OUTWARD in one direction (center to edge)
3. Use ONE STROKE per gauze
4. DISCARD gauze after single use
5. Take NEW gauze for next stroke
6. Continue from center outward until complete

Pattern Options:
• Linear: Center to left, center to right, center up, center down
• Circular: Start at center, spiral outward

ONE GAUZE = ONE STROKE = ONE DIRECTION

NEVER:
• Start from outside and move inward (WRONG - pushes bacteria into wound)
• Reuse the same gauze
• Scrub vigorously (gentle wiping only)
• Skip areas

WHY THIS DIRECTION:
Prevents dragging bacteria from surrounding skin into clean wound

AFTER CLEANING:
• Allow wound to air dry (1-2 minutes) OR
• Pat dry gently with clean sterile gauze
• Wound is now ready for dressing application

================================================================================
STEP 8: DISPOSE CONTAMINATED MATERIALS
================================================================================

WASTE DISPOSAL:

Used gauze, old dressing, gloves:
• Place in waste bag on bottom trolley shelf
• OR place in designated infectious waste bin (yellow/red)

Seal waste bag:
• When procedure complete
• Do not overfill

Remove gloves properly:
1. Pinch outside of one glove near wrist
2. Peel off, turning inside-out
3. Hold in gloved hand
4. Slide fingers under other glove
5. Peel off, creating bag with both gloves inside
6. Discard in waste

HAND HYGIENE:
• Wash hands immediately after glove removal
• Essential final step

================================================================================
COMMUNICATION WITH STAFF NURSE - SUMMARY
================================================================================

The student is expected to communicate with the staff nurse to verify:

FOR CLEANING SOLUTION:
• "Could you please verify this normal saline solution?"
• State: Name, expiration date, bottle integrity
• Wait for verbal confirmation

FOR STERILE DRESSING:
• "Could you please verify this sterile dressing packet?"
• State: Type, expiration date, package integrity
• Wait for verbal confirmation

This demonstrates:
• Professional responsibility
• Patient safety awareness
• Proper clinical protocol
• Effective communication

AGENTS SHOULD EVALUATE:
• Did student ASK staff nurse for verification?
• Did student clearly COMMUNICATE the details?
• Did student properly STATE the information (name, date, integrity)?
• Did student WAIT for confirmation before proceeding?

================================================================================
COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID
================================================================================

❌ MISTAKE: Not asking staff nurse for verification
✓ CORRECT: Always request staff nurse to check solution and dressing

❌ MISTAKE: Assuming verification without asking
✓ CORRECT: Explicitly ask for confirmation

❌ MISTAKE: Not stating details clearly
✓ CORRECT: State name, expiration date, integrity status

❌ MISTAKE: Proceeding without staff nurse approval
✓ CORRECT: Wait for verbal confirmation before using materials

❌ MISTAKE: Forgetting second hand wash (after trolley setup)
✓ CORRECT: Always wash hands twice (before trolley, after trolley)

❌ MISTAKE: Cleaning from outside toward wound
✓ CORRECT: Always clean center to periphery (outward)

❌ MISTAKE: Using same gauze for multiple strokes
✓ CORRECT: One gauze = one stroke, then discard

================================================================================
STEP SEQUENCE SUMMARY
================================================================================

PREPARATION PHASE:
1. ✓ Hand washing #1
2. ✓ Clean trolley with disinfectant
3. ✓ Arrange equipment on trolley (sterile top, waste bottom)
4. ✓ Hand washing #2
5. ✓ **COMMUNICATE with staff nurse: verify normal saline (name, date, integrity)**
6. ✓ **COMMUNICATE with staff nurse: verify sterile dressing (type, date, integrity)**
7. ✓ Bring trolley to patient area

CLEANING PHASE:
8. ✓ Hand hygiene + gloves on
9. ✓ Remove old dressing (if present)
10. ✓ Remove gloves, hand hygiene, new sterile gloves on
11. ✓ Prepare cleaning solution and gauze
12. ✓ Clean wound: CENTER TO PERIPHERY, one gauze per stroke
13. ✓ Allow wound to dry

COMPLETION PHASE:
14. ✓ Dispose all contaminated materials properly
15. ✓ Remove gloves correctly
16. ✓ Final hand hygiene

================================================================================
KEY PRINCIPLES
================================================================================

1. HAND HYGIENE IS EVERYTHING
   • Wash hands at every required step
   • Use proper 40-60 second technique

2. **COMMUNICATE WITH STAFF NURSE**
   • ASK for solution verification
   • ASK for dressing verification
   • STATE details clearly (name, expiration, integrity)
   • WAIT for confirmation

3. STERILITY MATTERS
   • Check expiration dates
   • Verify package integrity
   • Confirm with staff nurse

4. CENTER TO PERIPHERY
   • Always clean from wound center outward
   • Never reverse this direction

5. ONE GAUZE = ONE USE
   • Never reuse gauze
   • Prevents cross-contamination
