WOUND DRESSING PROTOCOL
For Clean Surgical Wounds

================================================================================
VR PROCEDURE STEPS
================================================================================

1. Perform hand washing
2. **ASK staff nurse to verify sterile dressing packet**
3. Apply dressing to wound
4. Perform hand washing again

================================================================================
STEP 1: HAND WASHING (Before Dressing)
================================================================================

WHEN: Before touching any sterile dressing materials

TECHNIQUE (40-60 seconds):
1. Wet hands with water
2. Apply soap
3. Rub palms together
4. Rub between fingers
5. Rub backs of hands
6. Rub thumbs
7. Rub fingertips
8. Rinse thoroughly
9. Dry with clean towel

WHY: Prevents contamination of sterile dressing

================================================================================
STEP 2: VERIFY DRESSING WITH STAFF NURSE
================================================================================

**CRITICAL COMMUNICATION STEP**

The student MUST ask the staff nurse to verify the sterile dressing packet.

WHAT THE STUDENT SHOULD DO:

1. APPROACH THE STAFF NURSE
   • Bring the dressing packet
   • Request verification
   • Professional demeanor

2. PRESENT THE DRESSING PACKET
   • Show the packet clearly to staff nurse
   • Allow nurse to inspect it
   • Point to relevant information

3. ASK FOR VERIFICATION
   Example questions student should ask:
   • "Could you please verify this sterile dressing packet?"
   • "Can you confirm this is the correct dressing?"
   • "Would you check the expiration date for me?"
   • "Is this package intact and safe to use?"
   • "Is this dressing appropriate for the wound?"

4. STATE THE DETAILS CLEARLY
   Example communication:
   • "This is a sterile dressing packet"
   • "It expires on [date]"
   • "The package appears intact with no tears"
   • "The seal is unbroken"

5. WAIT FOR STAFF NURSE CONFIRMATION
   • Listen to staff nurse feedback
   • Address any concerns raised
   • Get verbal approval: "Yes, you may proceed"
   • Thank the staff nurse

WHAT TO VERIFY TOGETHER:

✓ Package label: Says "Sterile Dressing"
✓ Expiration date: Must NOT be expired
✓ Package integrity: No tears, holes, or damage
✓ Package dry: No moisture or wetness
✓ Package seal: Completely sealed
✓ Appropriate size: Suitable for wound

NEVER USE IF:
• Package is torn or damaged
• Package is wet
• Expiration date has passed
• Staff nurse says not to use it
• You are unsure about sterility

WHY THIS COMMUNICATION MATTERS:

PATIENT SAFETY:
• Double-check prevents using contaminated materials
• Ensures only sterile dressings touch wounds
• Reduces infection risk

PROFESSIONAL PRACTICE:
• Demonstrates accountability
• Shows respect for supervision
• Follows clinical protocols
• Models safe nursing behavior

LEARNING:
• Staff nurse can correct errors
• Provides teaching opportunity
• Builds good habits for practice

EVALUATION CRITERIA - What agents should check:

✓ Did student approach staff nurse?
✓ Did student ask for dressing verification?
✓ Did student present the dressing packet clearly?
✓ Did student state dressing details (type, expiration, integrity)?
✓ Did student check package integrity?
✓ Did student wait for staff nurse confirmation?
✓ Did student demonstrate professional communication?

================================================================================
STEP 3: APPLY DRESSING
================================================================================

PREPARATION:
• Put on sterile gloves
• Ensure wound is clean and dry
• Open dressing packet (sterile technique - touch edges only)

DRESSING SIZE:
• Must cover entire wound
• Should extend 2-5 cm beyond wound edges
• Example: For 8 cm wound → use 10x10 cm dressing

APPLICATION STEPS:

1. Remove dressing from packet
   • Touch only the edges
   • Use sterile gloved hands
   • Do not touch center of dressing

2. Position dressing over wound
   • Center it over the wound
   • Covers all wound edges
   • One attempt only - do not reposition
   • If wrong position, get new sterile dressing

3. Secure dressing with tape (Picture Frame Method)
   • One tape strip across top edge
   • One tape strip across bottom edge
   • One tape strip on left side
   • One tape strip on right side
   • All four edges must be secured
   • No gaps or loose corners

4. Check the dressing
   ✓ Wound completely covered
   ✓ All edges taped down firmly
   ✓ Dressing lies flat (no wrinkles)
   ✓ No loose corners
   ✓ Extends 2-5 cm beyond wound

5. Label the dressing
   • Write date on tape: "29/01/2025"
   • Write time: "10:30"
   • Write your initials: "JD"

CRITICAL RULES:
• Hold dressing by edges only (never touch center)
• Place dressing in center of wound
• Do NOT reposition after placement (use new dressing if wrong)
• Secure all four edges with tape
• No gaps or loose edges allowed

================================================================================
STEP 4: HAND WASHING (After Dressing)
================================================================================

WHEN: After completing dressing application and removing gloves

STEPS:
1. Remove gloves properly:
   • Pinch outside of one glove
   • Peel off inside-out
   • Hold in gloved hand
   • Remove second glove inside-out
   • Discard both gloves
2. Wash hands thoroughly (40-60 seconds)
3. Dry hands with clean towel

WHY: Final infection control measure

================================================================================
PATIENT EDUCATION
================================================================================

After dressing applied, tell the patient:

• "Keep the dressing dry and clean"
• "Do not remove the dressing yourself"
• "Cover it when showering"
• "Tell us if the dressing gets wet or falls off"
• "Report any increased pain, redness, or fever"
• "We will change the dressing in [X] days"

WHY: Patient understanding improves outcomes

================================================================================
COMMUNICATION WITH STAFF NURSE - SUMMARY
================================================================================

**WHAT THE STUDENT MUST DEMONSTRATE:**

BEFORE DRESSING APPLICATION:
• Actively seek out staff nurse
• Request verification of dressing packet
• Clearly communicate the details:
  - Dressing type
  - Expiration date
  - Package integrity (intact/not damaged)
• Wait for verbal confirmation
• Respond appropriately to feedback

EXAMPLE CONVERSATION:

Student: "Excuse me, could you please verify this sterile dressing packet for me?"

[Shows packet to staff nurse]

Student: "This is a sterile wound dressing, it expires on February 15, 2025, and the package appears intact with no tears or moisture."

Staff Nurse: [Inspects packet] "Yes, that looks good. You may proceed."

Student: "Thank you."

THIS DEMONSTRATES:
• Professional communication
• Patient safety awareness
• Following clinical protocols
• Respect for supervision

AGENTS EVALUATE:
• Did student initiate communication with staff nurse?
• Did student ask for verification (not just assume)?
• Did student clearly state all key information?
• Did student wait for approval before proceeding?

================================================================================
COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID
================================================================================

**COMMUNICATION MISTAKES:**

❌ Not asking staff nurse at all
✓ Always request staff nurse verification

❌ Assuming verification without asking
✓ Explicitly ask: "Could you please verify this?"

❌ Not stating details clearly
✓ State: type, expiration date, package integrity

❌ Proceeding without waiting for approval
✓ Wait for verbal confirmation: "Yes, you may proceed"

❌ Mumbling or unclear communication
✓ Speak clearly and professionally

**PROCEDURAL MISTAKES:**

❌ Skipping hand washing before or after
✓ Always wash hands twice (before and after)

❌ Using expired or damaged dressing
✓ Check expiration and integrity first

❌ Touching center of sterile dressing
✓ Touch edges only

❌ Not securing all edges with tape
✓ Tape all four sides (picture frame method)

❌ Forgetting to label dressing
✓ Write date, time, initials on tape

KEY PRINCIPLES
================================================================================

1. **ASK STAFF NURSE FOR VERIFICATION**
   • Always request confirmation
   • State details clearly
   • Wait for approval

2. HAND HYGIENE
   • Before and after dressing application
   • Proper 40-60 second technique

3. STERILE TECHNIQUE
   • Touch edges only, never center
   • One placement attempt only

4. SECURE ALL EDGES
   • Tape all four sides
   • No gaps or loose corners

5. LABEL DRESSING
   • Date, time, initials
   • Accountability

6. PATIENT EDUCATION
   • Keep dry
   • Don't remove
   • When to report concerns
